Enchanted Matter is a design research practice dedicated to exploring and illuminating narratives of immigration and marginalized identities. Through the harmonious integration of computational and analog design, we aim to expose the unseen and foster a deeper understanding of diverse experiences.

There is a unique magic in this place—found in its vast skies, expansive landscapes, and most importantly, in the warmth of its people.

We aim to expose the unseen, fostering understanding of diverse experiences through the stories of the people.

Enchanted Matter (EM) was founded by Carlos Sabogal and Alaa Quraishi in 2022, with Zaqueriah Pacheco joining in 2024.

Transformative Makers

Team Members

A diverse team blending art, design, and technology to challenge norms and create meaningful cultural impact

Alaa Quraishi

Now a Visiting Assistant Professor at UNM, focuses her research on non-monetary feminine labor and marginalized voices. Her work draws on her diverse identity as a Muslim, Lebanese, Indian woman, and New Mexican, bringing fresh perspectives to her practice.

Carlos Sabogal

is a computational designer who has contributed to large-scale art and architectural projects. His home fabrication studio allows him to experiment with materials and design through cultural, environmental, and socio-political lenses, informed by his experience as a Colombian immigrant.

Zaqueriah Pacheco

is a skilled 3D designer and CNC machinist. His work includes major public and private art installations, such as sculptures at the Albuquerque Sunport and in Bend, OR. He aims to bring his technological expertise to even larger, impactful projects.
