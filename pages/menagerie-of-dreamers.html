Menagerie of DREAMers

DACA recipients, often referred to as DREAMers, are individuals brought to the U.S. as children without legal documentation. Under the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program, they are temporarily protected from deportation and granted work authorization, though their future remains uncertain due to the program's temporary nature.

The 'Menagerie of DREAMers' exhibit explores the experiences of DACA recipients through small-scale bedroom installations. Each room functions as a narrative space, filled with artifacts transitioning into a dream-like state that reflect DREAMers' stories. Custom-designed drawing devices allow viewers to engage by constructing and deconstructing elements of each story.

Digital craft, computational design, and research converge in each room, using architectural details to create an environment that feels both familiar and imaginative. The stories are illustrated through two-dimensional drawings superimposed onto a three-dimensional space, playing with the viewer's perception. Framed by domestic outlines punctuated with apertures like windows and door frames, these openings invite viewers to reinterpret DREAMers' lives.

Rooms displayed on varying-height stands encourage interaction, fostering intimacy and curiosity. Generic furniture represents DREAMers' uncertain status, while a key furniture piece in each room is abstracted and embedded with data on health, education, and economic conditions. This abstraction radiates through the room, visually representing how politics shape DACA recipients' lives, turning the furniture into a data visualization tool and the room into an active narrative.
